### **Tier 1: Pre-Print Publication (The First Move)**

For all three papers, our first step should be to publish them as pre-prints on **arXiv.org**.

* **Why?** It's the standard in computer science, physics, and mathematics. It gives us an immediate, publicly viewable, and timestamped version of our work. This establishes priority for our ideas while we navigate the slower peer-review process.
* **Action:** We can upload our drafted papers to the relevant `arXiv` categories. For example:
    * **Contextual Intelligence Hypothesis:** `cs.AI` (Artificial Intelligence), `cs.CL` (Computation and Language)
    * **The Ark Brain Design Pattern:** `cs.AI`, `cs.SE` (Software Engineering)
    * **Daoist Extension Theory:** `cs.CY` (Computation and Society), `nlin.AO` (Adaptation and Self-Organizing Systems)

### **Tier 2: Targeted Venues by Paper**

After posting to arXiv, we can submit to formal peer-reviewed venues. Each paper has a different ideal audience.
